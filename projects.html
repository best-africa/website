The technical Assistance included:

Building a strong and effective demand side: Organizational Capacity Building for three member-based farmer organisations (Ngakuyi Farmers Group, Iyete Farmers Group and Goruju Farmers’ Cooperative). To ensure that the organisations are successful in delivering the desired services to their members in terms of:

• Profiled the Products and service to be provided by the farmer organisation namely Input supply, Training and marketing
• Set up Processes: Financial and Nonfinancial Record Management Systems; Tools -Business Plan, policies and Procedures; Structures -Production and Natural Resource Management, Finance and Investment, Quality assurance marketing and value addition

The training was structured based on the farm business Cycle and delivered using farm business school approach.

Organisational capacity development: Empowered lending team with competences of efficiently and effectively delivering the agricultural loan product to the smallholder farmers

CLUSA International Northern Uganda Alutkot SACCO in Oyam, Ikwera SACCO in Apac, and Akalo SACCO in Kole. Sept 2014- May 2015

FACILITATING THE AGRO-RURAL COMMUNITY TO BE SELF-FINANCING BY MOBILIZING LOCAL FINANCIAL RESOURCES....
The overall purpose of the assignment was to strengthen the partners SACCOs northern region of Uganda namely Alutkot in Oyam, Ikwera in Apac, Akalo in Kole so that they are able to play their role of efficient and effective financial intermediaries facilitating the agro-rural community to be self-financing by mobilizing local financial resources (member share contributions and Savings) and attracting external investments from other working capital providers. It involved:

Updating the Governance Tools
• Updating a strategic business plan and developing 2014 annual work plan and quarter One and Quarter Two work plans
• Improving on the operations policies and procedures manual in the areas of human resource and governance, Credit and Savings management, Financial Management
• Conducting a SACCO Good governance and leadership Training for new Board members

Develop appropriate financial service strategy
• Conducting a two days’ work shop to identify the unmet financial life cycle and business needs long the different commodity value chains promoted under the Conservation Farming initiatives.
• Developing the Product Profiles of the Refined Products
• Training the board members and management staff in product Management

Improving the Quality of the Loan Portfolio in SACCOs
• Cleaning the existing Loan portfolio
• Developing and implementing Loan recovery strategies
• Training Board of Directors and management staff in Portfolio Management
• Conducting Customer care and Front Office Management training
• Coaching and mentoring board members especially the Loan committee in interpreting the loan portfolio report and using them in making recovery decisions and strategies
